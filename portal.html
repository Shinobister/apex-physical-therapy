Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our services, appointments, and billing.

What should I expect during my first visit?

+

Your first visit will include a comprehensive evaluation lasting approximately 60 minutes. We'll review your medical history, discuss your goals, perform a physical assessment, and develop a personalized treatment plan. Please bring your insurance card, photo ID, and any relevant medical records or imaging reports.

How many sessions will I need?

+

Treatment duration varies based on your condition, goals, and progress. Most patients attend 2-3 sessions per week for 4-8 weeks. We regularly reassess your progress and adjust your plan accordingly. Some patients may need longer-term care for chronic conditions, while others achieve their goals in just a few weeks.

Do I need a referral from my doctor?

+

In Connecticut, you have direct access to physical therapy without a physician referral for up to 6 visits or 30 days, whichever comes first. After that, we'll need a referral to continue treatment. Some insurance plans require a referral from the start—we'll help you verify your benefits before your first visit.

What should I wear to my appointments?

+

Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows access to the area being treated. For lower body issues, shorts or loose pants are ideal. For upper body issues, a tank top or loose shirt works well. We have private changing rooms available if needed.

How do you handle billing and insurance?

+

We verify your insurance benefits before your first visit and bill your insurance directly for covered services. You are responsible for any copays, deductibles, or non-covered services at the time of your visit. We accept most major insurance plans and offer flexible payment options for self-pay patients.
